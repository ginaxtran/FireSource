Low Risk (Minimal Fire Danger)
  • Continue general fire safety practices.
  • Have basic emergency plan in case conditions change.
  • Outdoor burning may be permitted; check local regulations.
Medium Risk (Elevated Fire Danger)
  • Use caution with open flames, sparks or outdoor burning.
  • Maintain at least 30 feet of defensible space around structures.
  • Monitor weather updates and fire reports.
High Risk (Increased Fire Danger)
  • Be ready to evacuate at short notice.
  • Watch out for flammable materials (dry leaves, woodpiles) from buildings.
  • Look out for emergency alerts and real-time fire warnings.
